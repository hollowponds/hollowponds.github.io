A New Big Book Is Out Now: The Last Mans Guide to the Lone Guard Research Zone

I’m very happy to say that The Last Mans Guide to the Lone Guard Research Zone is out now on DriveThruRPG.

This is one of my new big books, and I’m genuinely pleased to be releasing something of this size again. It’s been a while since I put out a larger guide like this, and it feels good to be back working on books that let me really dig into a location, its tone, its secrets, and the kind of stories it can create at the table.

Some of you may remember me mentioning Lone Guard in the Rifts videos. It was always one of those places that sat in the background with a lot of implied danger and mystery, but we never actually went there. This book changes that.

The Lone Guard Research Zone is a place of false normality, quiet control, old science, and hidden horror. The towns are still running. The streets are still swept. The people still smile. But beneath that surface something is deeply wrong, and deep below it all waits the Wardein Complex, full of locked doors, strange experiments, and secrets that should have stayed buried.

This book gives you a large location to explore, a linked adventure site, and plenty of tools to help bring it to life at the table. There are verbs, prompts, encounters, missions, rumours, enemy ideas, and the kind of material that helps solo players and GMs keep play moving while still leaving room for surprise and discovery. It is built to be useful, playable, and full of atmosphere.

I think it’s one of the strongest location books I’ve done, and I’m proud of how it came together.

So, if you enjoy post-apocalyptic mystery, science gone wrong, creeping horror, and locations with a lot of story potential, I’d love for you to take a look.
