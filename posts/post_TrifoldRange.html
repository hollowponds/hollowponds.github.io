The Trifold Range Is Growing, and I Love What It’s Becoming

The trifold range keeps growing, and honestly, I’m really pleased with how useful these have become.

I love this format because it does exactly what I want a game aid to do. It’s small, focused, and practical. Whether you’re a solo player or a DM, a good tool should help you play, not give you more homework.

That’s the whole point of these trifolds.

They’re built to be used at the table. Printed out, folded up, kept beside you, and reached for when you need a nudge, a structure, a prompt, or a way to get unstuck. They’re there to help you move play forward.

For solo players, that matters a lot. Solo play can stall when there’s too much to hold in your head at once. Sometimes you do not need more theory. You need something clear and usable that helps you answer the next question and keep going.

For DMs, it’s much the same. Running a game means juggling ideas, pacing, NPCs, surprises, and the weird directions players always head in. A trifold can sit beside your notes and give you support without taking over the session.

That’s why I keep making them.

As the range grows, it’s becoming a proper toolkit: different trifolds doing different jobs, all aimed at helping you play more easily, prep more quickly, and keep the story moving.

That’s what I want my tools to do. Be useful. Be clear. Be something you actually come back to.
